PRELLI VISION

PRELLI envisions economically empowered, socially uplifted, culturally and morally upright individuals and communities collectively committed to become effective catalysts for progress not only in Tarlac Province but also for the entire country.

PRELLI MISSION

PRELLI aims to help alleviate the socio-economic conditions of the less-fortunate thru agro-industrial programs; to be an instrument in empowering and preserving the culture of its beneficiaries; to push for a genuine moral recovery program; to promote education thru the introduction of relevant and effective literacy programs; to campaign for the protection of the environment; and to continuously uphold human dignity by helping provide the basic need for food, clothing and shelter.

PRELLI FOUNDATION PROJECT'S

"Lingap sa Katutubo" is a project by the Prelli Foundation that focuses on providing essential support to indigenous communities. The project aims to address the basic needs of these communities by distributing food, shirts, and slippers. With a deep commitment to alleviating the challenges faced by indigenous people, the Prelli Foundation's initiative ensures that these groups, often living in remote or underserved areas, have access to vital resources. The project not only helps meet immediate needs but also contributes to improving the dignity and well-being of the indigenous population, promoting their welfare through acts of kindness and generosity.

SEC Certificate of Incorporation

A SEC Certificate of Incorporation refers to the official document issued by the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) of the Philippines, acknowledging the creation of a corporation. In the Philippines, the SEC is the government agency responsible for regulating business entities, including corporations.
When a company decides to incorporate, it must file specific documents with the SEC, including the Articles of Incorporation. Once the SEC reviews and approves the company's submission, it issues a Certificate of Incorporation. This certificate serves as proof that the corporation is legally recognized and allowed to operate in the country.

CERTIFICATE OF FILLING ADAPTATION OF NEW BY-LAWS

A Certificate of Filing Adaptation of New By-Laws typically refers to an official document issued by a government agency, regulatory body, or corporation confirming that a company, organization, or association has filed its updated or newly adapted by-laws with the relevant authorities.

SEC Certificate of Incorporation

A SEC Certificate of Incorporation is an official document issued by the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) that certifies the legal existence of a corporation. It is provided after the corporation has successfully registered with the SEC and met all the necessary requirements for incorporation under the laws of the relevant jurisdiction.

CERTIFICATE OF TAX EXEMPTION

a Certificate of Tax Exemption is an official document issued by the Bureau of Internal Revenue (BIR) or other relevant government agencies that confirms an organization or entity is exempt from paying certain taxes. This exemption is usually granted to specific organizations, institutions, or individuals based on their legal status or activities.

CERTIFICATE OF DSWD REGISTRATION

A Certificate of DSWD Registration is an official document issued by the Department of Social Welfare and Development (DSWD) in the Philippines, certifying that a particular organization, institution, or entity has been officially registered with the DSWD and is authorized to carry out specific social welfare activities.
